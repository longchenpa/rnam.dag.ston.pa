I would like to express my concerns about certain aspects of the Homotopy Type Theory (HoTT) community. When I attempted to discuss the Russian invasion with a HoTT group, my message was deleted, and I was banned. At the same time, I have seen members from Brazil making deeply inappropriate remarks about Ukraine in February 2022. Given these experiences, I find it difficult to support funding initiatives for researchers who collaborate with individuals engaged in unethical behavior.

Regarding the development of HoTT, it is important to acknowledge that contributions to the field have come from many individuals. While Vladimir Voevodsky played a major role in popularizing Univalence, substantial progress was made after 2016, particularly by Swedish researchers. The mathematical foundations of HoTT were not solely built by one person but rather by a broader community of early contributors.

Moreover, I am concerned about the lack of recognition for significant technical achievements. For example, despite having developed the fastest cubical type checker, my work has not been acknowledged in related research. In contrast, projects such as András Kovács' type checker, which is not as fast, receive more attention. This raises broader questions about fairness and transparency in academic citation practices.

Finally, I want to highlight the differences in research funding structures. In Ukraine, researchers often need to secure their own funding for projects and conference travel. Meanwhile, grants in other countries, such as the U.S., provide substantial financial support to researchers, sometimes exceeding hundreds of thousands of dollars. These disparities create challenges for those outside well-funded institutions.

I hope for a more ethical, inclusive, and merit-based approach in both the academic and research communities.
